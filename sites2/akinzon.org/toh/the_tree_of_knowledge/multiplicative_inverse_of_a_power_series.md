# Multiplicative Inverse of a power series

A polynomial $B(x)$ is an inverse of $A(x)$ if $A(x)B(x) = 1.$

For example, $1-x$ is an inverse of $1 + x + x^2 + x^3 + · · ·$ since it is the same as $\frac{1}{1-x}$

In general, we have:
$$ 1 + rx + r^2x^2 + . . . = \frac{1}{1-rx} $$
